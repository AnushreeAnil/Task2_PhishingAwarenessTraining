01 · Understand the threat

What is phishing?

Phishing is a social-engineering attack in which criminals impersonate a trusted person or organization to steal passwords, payment details, one-time codes, or sensitive data.

Impersonation

Attackers copy familiar brands, coworkers, and public agencies to appear trustworthy.

Deception

Messages manipulate emotions so that you act before checking the facts.

Data theft

Fake forms and login pages capture credentials and confidential information.

02 · Know the impact

Why is phishing dangerous?

One convincing message can harm a person, a business, or an entire supply chain.

Financial Theft

Stolen banking details enable fraudulent purchases and transfers.

Identity Theft

Personal data can be reused to open accounts or commit fraud.

Corporate Compromise

One account may unlock internal data, systems, and customers.

Malware & Ransomware

Links and attachments can install destructive malicious software.

03 · Recognize the methods

Types of phishing

The channel and target may change, but the objective remains the same: earning your trust.

01

Email Phishing

Mass-distributed fraudulent emails that imitate recognized brands.

02

Spear Phishing

Personalized attacks researched and tailored to one target.

03

Whaling

Spear phishing aimed at executives and other high-value leaders.

04

Business Email Compromise

Impersonated executives or vendors request money or sensitive data.

05

Clone Phishing

A real message is copied, then its link or attachment is replaced.

06

Smishing

Malicious text messages pose as delivery, bank, or account alerts.

07

Vishing

Fraudulent calls use conversation and caller-ID spoofing.

08

Angler Phishing

Fake support accounts target complaints posted on social media.

05 · Inspect before you sign in

Fake website detection

Choose the fake website before revealing the evidence.

Website A
https://accounts.company.com
  • Correct domain with no extra words
  • HTTPS protects data in transit
  • Valid certificate for the domain
  • Clear writing and real contact details
VS
Website B
http://company-login-prize.xyz
  • Domain spoofing with added words
  • No HTTPS or misleading lock imagery
  • Grammar errors and broken navigation
  • No contact details and unrealistic offers

Website B is fake: its unrelated .xyz domain, missing HTTPS, poor writing, absent contact information, and unrealistic offer are red flags. HTTPS means a connection is encrypted—not that a site is honest.

06 · Understand manipulation

The psychology of social engineering

Hover or focus on each card to see the safest response to a common emotional trigger.

Urgency

“Act in the next 10 minutes.”

Pause the clock.

Deadlines are designed to suppress careful thinking. Verify through an official channel.

Fear

“Your account has been hacked.”

Check independently.

Open the official app or type the known address yourself instead of using the message.

Trust

“I’m from your support team.”

Trust, then verify.

Confirm the identity using a number or directory you already know.

Authority

“The CEO needs this now.”

Follow procedure.

Seniority never overrides payment, privacy, or security controls.

    10 · Knowledge check

    Phishing awareness quiz

    Choose one answer for every question, then submit your training assessment.

    01 Which is the strongest warning sign in a login link?

    A misspelled or unrelated domain means the login is not hosted by the organization it claims to represent.

    02 What should you do with an urgent payment request from your CEO?

    Use a known phone number or in-person confirmation; replying stays inside a potentially compromised channel.

    03 What is “smishing”?

    Smishing combines SMS and phishing: deceptive text messages lead victims to fraudulent calls or websites.

    04 Does HTTPS alone prove a website is legitimate?

    Criminals can obtain certificates too. HTTPS protects transit, while the complete domain identifies the site.

    05 What should you do after identifying a phishing message at work?

    Reporting helps security teams block the campaign and protect colleagues who received the same message.
